What Adobe structurally cannot deliver

Not "Adobe is bad" - capability gaps: features that do not exist in Adobe Analytics today, regardless of effort or budget. This is the new ground Vi gains by switching.

Capability
Adobe Analytics today
With Contentsquare
1 Event collection
✕ Manual tagging plan blocks every release. Engineers maintain hundreds of tags; missed tags = data gaps.
✓ Smart Capture records all clicks, taps, scrolls, forms, errors - no code, no plan. Retroactive analysis of past sessions.
2 Session replay
✕ No native replay. Adobe shows aggregate funnels and drops, never the actual user session.
✓ Session Replay with auto-tagging of frustration signals, rage-clicks, error sessions. AI summarises behaviour across up to 100 sessions.
3 AI insights
✕ Adobe Sensei flags anomalies - but you still investigate. No natural-language interface.
✓ Sense Analyst answers questions in plain English, generates session summaries, writes optimisation recommendations.
4 Zoning & heatmaps
✕ Add-on only via Target / 3rd-party; no native revenue-per-zone attribution.
✓ Zoning Analysis attributes engagement, exposure, & revenue to every element on a page - reading rate, attractiveness, hesitation time.
5 Error → revenue link
✕ JS errors are not natively quantified in revenue terms. Triage requires manual correlation.
✓ Find & Fix Real-Time shows the live error, impacted sessions, and revenue at risk - automatically.
6 Self-serve for business
✕ Reporting requires SQL, dashboard build skills, or an analyst-in-the-loop. PMs queue for answers.
✓ Plain-language questions in Sense, drag-and-drop journeys, pre-built telco templates.
7 Pricing model
✕ Server-call / impression-based - incentives push teams to under-tag to stay in budget.
✓ Session-based - capturing more events doesn't cost more. 100% of events, always.
